World War I: A Cosmic Journey Through History

World War I, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict that lasted from 1914 to 1918. It reshaped the world's political landscape and had far-reaching consequences that continue to influence our world today.

1914: The Spark

June 28: Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo
July 28: Austria-Hungary declares war on Serbia
August 1-4: Germany, Russia, France, and Britain join the war

1915: Trench Warfare

February: Germany begins unrestricted submarine warfare
April 22: Second Battle of Ypres, first large-scale use of poison gas
May 7: Sinking of the Lusitania

1916: Battles of Attrition

February-December: Battle of Verdun
July-November: Battle of the Somme
May 31-June 1: Battle of Jutland

1917: New Developments

April 6: United States enters the war
November 7: Bolshevik Revolution in Russia
November 20: Battle of Cambrai, first large-scale use of tanks

1918: The Final Push

March 21: Germany launches Spring Offensive
July 18: Allied counter-offensive begins
November 11: Armistice signed, ending the war

Key Facts and Figures

  • Total military deaths: ~9.7 million
  • Total civilian deaths: ~10 million
  • Countries involved: 32
  • Cost: ~$208 billion (in 1913 dollars)

Major Consequences

  • Collapse of four empires: German, Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, and Russian
  • Creation of new nation-states in Europe
  • Rise of communism in Russia
  • Seeds planted for World War II
  • Advancement in military technology
  • Women's suffrage movements gained momentum
