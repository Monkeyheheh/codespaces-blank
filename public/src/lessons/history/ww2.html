World War II: A Cosmic Journey Through History

World War II was a global conflict that lasted from 1939 to 1945. It involved most of the world's nations and was the deadliest and most destructive war in history, reshaping the global political landscape and leading to significant technological advancements.

1939: The Beginning

September 1: Germany invades Poland
September 3: Britain and France declare war on Germany
September 17: Soviet Union invades Poland

1940: Axis Advances

April-June: Germany invades Denmark, Norway, Belgium, the Netherlands, and France
July-October: Battle of Britain
September 27: Tripartite Pact signed (Germany, Italy, Japan)

1941: Global Expansion

June 22: Germany invades the Soviet Union
December 7: Japan attacks Pearl Harbor
December 8-11: US enters the war

1942-1943: Turning Points

June 4-7, 1942: Battle of Midway
August 1942-February 1943: Battle of Stalingrad
November 1942: Operation Torch (North Africa)

1944-1945: Allied Victory

June 6, 1944: D-Day (Normandy Invasion)
August 25, 1944: Liberation of Paris
May 7, 1945: Germany surrenders
August 6 & 9, 1945: Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki
September 2, 1945: Japan surrenders, war ends

Key Facts and Figures

  • Total military deaths: ~24 million
  • Total civilian deaths: ~45 million
  • Countries involved: Over 30
  • Cost: Estimated $1 trillion (in 1945 dollars)

Major Consequences

  • Formation of the United Nations
  • Beginning of the Cold War
  • Decolonization and independence movements
  • Nuremberg Trials and Tokyo Trials
  • Technological advancements (radar, jet engines, nuclear technology)
  • Economic and social changes, including women's increased participation in the workforce
